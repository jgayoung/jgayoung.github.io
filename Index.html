About [empath] QUARTERLY

[empath]QUARTERLY is a literary magazine dedicated to raising funds for various non-profit organizations and charities. It was founded by Jenny Jung in December of 2013.
75% of all proceeds earned from selling print copies of Volume I will be donated to the Massachusetts Alliance on Teen Pregnancy, while the remaining 25% is being saved to help fund the costs of printing Volume II. Whether you support us by submitting your work, helping spread the word, or by buying a copy of [empath] QUARTERLY, know that you are supporting a great cause!
[empath] is about snapshots. Snapshots of humanity at its best, its worst, its most ordinary. We publish visual art and writing by people of all ages.

Submit to Volume II of [empath] QUARTERLY!

Deadline: September 2, 2014

At [empath] QUARTERLY, we look for the quietly dented, slightly chipped soul of humanity that slinks within all of us. We focus on compassion and empathy, although we certainly wonâ€™t sneeze at purposely cynical works. Does the creation make us feel? Is it cathartic? Will the Editor-in-Chief have the urge to eat ice cream and sob into her pillows after reading/looking at your piece?
[empath] wants the pieces that show us what it means to be human. If you have a piece you would like to share with us, please submit.

Poetry

Single-Spaced
Times New Roman
12-Point Font
Under 200 Lines

To submit your work, send an email to empathquarterly@gmail.com with the subject line [poetry submission]. Your submission should be attached as a .doc/.docx/.pdf file.
In the body of the email, please include your name, school (if you attend one), grade (if you are still a student), state, and a brief bio of under 130 words. Should your submission be accepted, this information will be included in the issue.

Prose

Double-Spaced
Times New Roman
12-Point Font
Under 3500 Words

To submit your work, send an email to empathquarterly@gmail.com with the subject line as either [short story submission] or [creative non-fiction submission], depending on which category your piece falls under. Your submission should be attached as a .doc/.docx/.pdf file.
In the body of the email, please include your name, school (if you attend one), grade (if you are still a student), state, and a brief bio of under 130 words. Should your submission be accepted, this information will be included in the issue.

Script

Times New Roman
12-Point Font
Under 10 Pages


To submit your work, send an email to empathquarterly@gmail.com with the subject line [drama submission]. Your submission should be attached as a .doc/.docx/.pdf file.
In the body of the email, please include your name, school (if you attend one), grade (if you are still a student), state, and a brief bio of under 130 words. Should your submission be accepted, this information will be included in the issue.

Academic Essay

Double-Spaced
Times New Roman
12-Point Font
Under 3500 Words
All works must be cited.

To submit your work, send an email to empathquarterly@gmail.com with the subject line [academic submission]. Your submission should be attached as a .doc/.docx/.pdf file, and it should include a works cited page.
In the body of the email, please include your name, school (if you attend one), grade (if you are still a student), state, and a brief bio of under 130 words. Should your submission be accepted, this information will be included in the issue.

Musical Composition

Original compositions written for any instrument.

To submit your work, send an email to empathquarterly@gmail.com with the subject line [music submission]. Your submission should be attached as a jpeg/.pdf file.
In the body of the email, please include your name, school (if you attend one), grade (if you are still a student), state, and a brief bio of under 130 words. Should your submission be accepted, this information will be included in the issue.

Visual Art

Paintings, drawings, photography, etc.
Unless accepted as cover art, will be printed in black and white.

To submit your work, send an email to empathquarterly@gmail.com with the subject line [visual art submission]. Your submission should be attached as a .jpeg file.
In the body of the email, please include your name, school (if you attend one), grade (if you are still a student), state, and a brief bio of under 130 words. Should your submission be accepted, this information will be included in the issue.
